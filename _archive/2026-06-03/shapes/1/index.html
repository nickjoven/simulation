parabola — y = x² + μ

μ = −0.36

x² + μ = 0 ⇒ x = ±√(−μ) for μ < 0 0 for μ = 0 (saddle-node) (no real root) for μ > 0

identity

The parabola is the lowest-degree polynomial that has a minimum. Slide μ down: two roots open out like ±√(−μ). Slide μ up: they collide at the origin and vanish. That collision is the saddle-node bifurcation, the only generic way a fixed point can be born or destroyed on the line.

role in the framework

The square-root branch Δx ∝ √ε is the source of the exponent 2 in the Born rule. At every Arnold-tongue boundary of the circle map, the stable and unstable fixed points sit at ±√ε; the tongue width is ∝ Δx² = |ψ|². The exponent is not a postulate — it is the structural stability of the saddle-node normal form.
