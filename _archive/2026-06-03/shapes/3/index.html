half-twist — ψ(x + L) = −ψ(x)

τ = 0.50 (Möbius)

antiperiodic: ψ(x + L) = e^{i π τ} · ψ(x) τ = 0 → periodic (Hückel ring) τ = ½ → −1, one half-twist (Möbius) τ = 1 → +1, full twist (back to periodic)

identity

A strip glued end-to-end with one half-rotation. Walk around once, your sign flips. Walk around twice, you return. The twist is a global property — it cannot be removed by relabeling the local basis. It is a topological invariant: det M = −1, where M is the Fibonacci recursion matrix [[1,1],[1,0]].

role in the framework

The framework's Klein-bottle boundary condition is the half-twist applied to the substrate's phase field. It produces three consequences that the periodic ring does not:

  • Frustration. The coupling wants all phases equal; the topology demands a π jump somewhere. The jump migrates to the widest gap (1/φ) — see page 8.
  • Mode parity. Only modes with the right denominator survive — q even vs odd matters.
  • Spectral tilt. The half-twist forces the recursion det M = −1, which gives the φ² self-similarity of the spectral tilt.
