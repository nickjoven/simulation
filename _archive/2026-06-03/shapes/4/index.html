circle map — θ' = θ + Ω − (K/2π) sin 2π θ

K = 0.60

tongue p/q = { Ω : circle map mode-locks at rotation p/q } width w(p/q) ∝ K^q / q² (small-K asymptotic)

identity

For each rational p/q in [0, 1], there is a range of driving frequencies Ω that locks the map's average rotation to p/q. Those ranges are Arnold tongues. As K grows the tongues widen; at K = 1 they fill the circle and the gaps shrink to a Cantor set of measure zero. K = 1 is the framework's full-synchronization limit; K < 1 is the partial-synchronization regime.

role in the framework

Three load-bearing facts live here:

  • Saddle-node at every edge. Every tongue boundary is the parabola of page 1. Δθ ∝ √ε, so tongue width ∝ Δθ² — this is the Born rule.
  • q² weighting. Wider for small q. The Stern-Brocot tree's hierarchy is inheritance of this asymptotic.
  • K = 1 ↔ K < 1. K = 1 continuum limit gives Einstein. K < 1 continuum limit gives Schrödinger. The split is not smooth; it is the N11 tongue-coverage discontinuity.
