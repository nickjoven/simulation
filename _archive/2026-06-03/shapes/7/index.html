Klein bottle — two half-twists

u = 0.00

K² = S¹ × S¹ / ( (φ, θ) ∼ (φ+π, −θ) ) ↑ Möbius identification in one direction 4 modes survive at (q₂, q₃) = (2, 3) under Z/2 ⊕ Z/2 (XOR selection).

identity

Take a torus, then glue one pair of edges with a flip. The result is the Klein bottle: a closed non-orientable surface. Equivalently: two Möbius strips glued along their boundaries. The single number that classifies it is Z/2 for orientability, plus a Z/2 ⊕ Z/2 for the (q₂, q₃) = (2, 3) mode parity.

role in the framework

The Klein-bottle topology selects exactly four mode classes from the Stern-Brocot tree at the smallest denominators:

  • q₂² − 1 = q₃ (2² − 1 = 3): the cross-link identity that forces (q₂, q₃) to be (2, 3).
  • q₃² − 1 = q₂³ (3² − 1 = 2³): the dual identity. Both must hold; this picks (2, 3) uniquely from the integers.
  • Standard Model. The four mode sectors map onto the gauge group SU(3) × SU(2) × U(1) and produce three generations. See klein_bottle.md.
