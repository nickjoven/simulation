locking — a 1/q-locked orbit is a regular q-gon in S¹

q = 3 · triangle   p = 1

locked at rotation ρ = p/q ⇔ the orbit visits exactly q points θₖ = 2π · (k · p mod q) / q, k = 0,…,q−1 the q points are the vertices of a regular q-gon inscribed in S¹.

identity

If an oscillator is phase-locked to rational rotation number p/q, it returns to its starting phase after exactly q kicks, having visited q distinct phases. Those q phases are spaced uniformly around the unit circle — they are the vertices of a regular q-gon. The integer p tells you the winding: how many times around the circle the orbit travels in those q steps. p = 1 walks the vertices in order; p = 2 skips every other one (a star polygon for gcd(p,q) = 1).

role in the framework

Mode-locking is the framework's fundamental gluing operation — every load-bearing structure rides on it:

  • Born rule. The tongue at p/q has width ∝ K^q/q²; q² weighting comes from the q-gon's symmetry under Z/q.
  • (q₂, q₃) = (2, 3). The Klein-bottle topology selects the two smallest q-gons: the line segment (q = 2) and the triangle (q = 3). That is the gauge-group seed.
  • Stick-slip ratio. The Stribeck-lattice N = 3 critical length is the triangle becoming the first stable polygon.
