mediant — a/b ⊕ c/d = (a+c)/(b+d), as vectors

 1/2 ⊕ 1/3 = 2/5

Stern (1858), Brocot (1861): a/b ⊕ c/d = (a+c)/(b+d) neighbours a/b < c/d in some F_n ⇒ bc − ad = 1 (unimodular) the mediant is the unique simplest fraction strictly between them.

identity

Place two unit phasors at angles 2π·a/b and 2π·c/d on the circle. Their tip-to-tail sum is a vector whose angle lies between those two — specifically, in the angular wedge containing 2π·(a+c)/(b+d). That is the mediant. The picture is the proof that the mediant lives strictly between its parents.

The unimodular condition bc − ad = 1 means the two unit phasors, combined with the origin, form a triangle of area exactly 1/2. The mediant's denominator b + d is the magnitude of the diagonal of the parallelogram they span — that is why deeper-tree fractions have larger denominators and shorter resultant vectors.

role in the framework

  • Energy + stability ⇒ mediant. The mediant is not assumed. It is the unique combining rule that conserves energy and preserves Arnold-tongue stability at all depths. See mediant_derivation.md.
  • SL(2, ℤ) generation. The matrix [[a,c],[b,d]] with bc − ad = 1 lives in SL(2, ℤ); the mediant is the addition of its columns. Repeated mediants tile the Stern-Brocot tree, whose boundary is ℚ ∪ {∞} = ∂ℍ.
  • Three dimensions. SL(2, ℝ) is the continuum closure of SL(2, ℤ). Its dimension is 3. See three_dimensions.md.
