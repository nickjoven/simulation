frustration — three spins, antiperiodic edges, 120° resolution

t = 0.00 (initial)  

three oscillators on a Möbius triangle: edge cost C(θᵢ, θⱼ) = − cos(θᵢ − θⱼ + π) (antiferromagnetic: π offset preferred) no assignment can satisfy all three edges → frustration. the minimum-cost assignment is θ = (0, 2π/3, 4π/3): a regular triangle.

identity

Three sites; each pair of edges wants the two spins π apart. On a loop of three edges, you cannot have all three pairs antialigned — walk around and you accumulate 3π = π (mod 2π), not 0. The system is geometrically frustrated. It relaxes to the next-best configuration: three spins at 0, 2π/3, 4π/3 — exactly the triangle of page 1, exactly the q = 3 Arnold tongue, exactly the half-twist gap centred at 1/φ.

role in the framework

The Klein-bottle BC creates the same kind of frustration on a global scale: the coupling wants all phases equal, the topology demands a π jump, the system places that jump where it costs least.

  • Where the twist lives. The 1/φ gap between Arnold tongues 1/2 and 1/3 is the free spot — frustration is cheapest there.
  • 120° as the natural alternative to 180°. When pairwise anti-alignment fails, three-fold symmetry takes over. This is the geometric reason q = 3 is forced alongside q = 2 in the Klein bottle.
  • Stick-slip N = 3. The Stribeck-lattice critical chain length is 3 — the shortest chain on which the system can host a frustration triangle.
