sine — y = sin(2π θ)

K = 1.00

θ' = θ + Ω − (K / 2π) · sin(2π θ) (the circle map)

identity

One period, one bump up, one bump down, exactly antisymmetric about the half-turn. That symmetry — sin(θ + π) = −sin θ — is why a half-twist boundary condition (next page) leaves the equation intact while flipping the sign of every locked state.

role in the framework

The coupling term in every oscillator equation in the framework is sin(ψ − θ): Kuramoto, the circle map, the Stribeck chain. Two consequences:

  • Arnold tongues. The factor K · sin folds the rotation number onto rationals p/q in width ∝ K^q / q² near K = 0.
  • Half-period symmetry. sin(2π(θ+1/2)) = −sin(2π θ). The k → k + 1/2 shift of the orbital quantum number on a half-Möbius ring is exactly this identity in disguise.
