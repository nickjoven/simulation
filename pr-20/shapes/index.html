shapes · what the framework is made of

Eight elementary shapes carry the framework. Each one is a visual you can draw in seconds; each one is doing load-bearing work in a derivation on harmonics. Teaching material — the visual first, the equation second, the role in the framework last.

  1. parabola  y = x² · saddle-node normal form · |ψ|² exponent
  2. sine  y = sin x · circle map coupling · phase response
  3. half-twist  1/2 · ψ(x+L) = −ψ(x) · Möbius, det M = −1
  4. circle map · Arnold tongues  θ' = θ + Ω − (K/2π) sin 2πθ · saddle-node at every tongue edge
  5. devil's staircase  rotation number ρ(Ω) · self-similar at φ²
  6. Farey · Stern-Brocot  mediant (p₁+p₂)/(q₁+q₂) · widths ∝ 1/q²
  7. Klein bottle  two half-twists · 4 modes at (q₂,q₃) = (2,3)
  8. golden gap  1/φ between tongues 1/2 and 1/3 · where the twist lives

the one-paragraph through-line

A rational p/q on a circle is an Arnold tongue. Every tongue boundary is a saddle-node bifurcation — the parabola y = x²+μ collapsing two fixed points. The basin separation is Δθ ∝ √ε, so the tongue width — equivalently the basin measure — is ∝ Δθ² = |ψ|². That is the Born rule. The Mediant builds the rationals; the Farey partition orders them; their widths sum to 1. A half-twist at the widest gap (1/φ, between q=2 and q=3) is the Möbius boundary condition; two half-twists are the Klein bottle. Everything else — the spectral tilt, the Standard Model gauge group, Einstein at K = 1, Schrödinger at K < 1 — is bookkeeping on these shapes.
