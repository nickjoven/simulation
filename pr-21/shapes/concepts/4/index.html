Kuramoto order — r·e^{iψ} = (1/N) Σ e^{iθₖ}

K = 0.80   N = 24  

order parameter: r · e^{iψ} = (1/N) · Σₖ e^{iθₖ} phase distribution: θₖ = ψ + (1 − K) · ηₖ , ηₖ ∼ uniform[−π, π] |r| ∈ [0, 1]: 0 = incoherent, 1 = full synchronization (Einstein limit)

identity

Put N oscillators on the circle. Their centroid in the disk is a single complex number r·e^{iψ}: r tells you how synchronized they are, ψ tells you the mean phase. r = 0 means the arrows cancel — every direction equally occupied, no coherence. r = 1 means every arrow points the same way — total sync. Real systems hover somewhere in between.

role in the framework

  • Continuum limit at K = 1. When r = 1, every oscillator is locked. The rational field equation collapses to the Einstein field equations — the unique outcome by Lovelock's theorem. See einstein_from_kuramoto.md.
  • Continuum limit at K < 1. When r < 1, gaps remain. The continuum limit gives Schrödinger. Same equation, different regime. See continuum_limits.md.
  • Born rule. P(state k) = r_k² is the disk-centroid picture of born_rule.md: the basin measure is the squared amplitude of the synchronization vector toward attractor k.
